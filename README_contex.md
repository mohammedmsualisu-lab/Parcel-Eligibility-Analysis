🌱 Carbon Farming Project — Context & Rationale
📌 Overview
Carbon farming is an agricultural approach that promotes the adoption of sustainable land management practices to increase carbon sequestration in soils.
These practices contribute to:
•	Improved soil health and fertility 
•	Reduced reliance on agricultural inputs 
•	Increased or stabilized yields under climate variability 
•	Additional income for farmers through carbon credit markets 
This project is designed in alignment with standards from Verra, ensuring environmental integrity and credibility in carbon credit generation.

🎯 Project Objectives
•	Enable farmers to adopt regenerative agricultural practices 
•	Generate high-quality carbon credits 
•	Ensure compliance with international certification standard 
•	Support climate mitigation and rural economic development 

🧩 Key Project Components
1. MRV (Monitoring, Reporting, and Verification)
A core requirement in carbon projects is the ability to:
•	Monitor practice adoption 
•	Quantify carbon sequestration 
•	Report outcomes transparently 
•	Verify results through independent audits 

2. Parcel Eligibility Assessment
Not all land can be included in a carbon farming project. Each parcel must meet strict eligibility criteria based on environmental and regulatory constraints.

✅ Eligibility Criteria
A parcel is considered eligible only if:
•	It is not located within protected areas 
•	It does not overlap with forests, wetlands, or water bodies 
•	It is not in urban or built-up areas 
•	It complies with national climate policies (NDCs) 
•	It is suitable for sustainable agricultural practices 
Parcels violating any of these constraints risk project rejection during certification.

⚠️ Key Risks Addressed
•	Inclusion of non-compliant land 
•	Certification delays or rejection 
•	Inaccurate carbon credit estimation 
•	Regulatory conflicts with environmental protections 

🌍 Importance of This Work
This eligibility analysis is a critical early-stage filter that ensures:
•	Only valid parcels enter the project pipeline 
•	Certification processes run smoothly 
•	Environmental safeguards are respected 

📈 Expected Impact
•	Stronger credibility of carbon credits 
•	Increased farmer participation and trust 
•	Scalable project deployment across regions 
•	Contribution to national and global climate goals
